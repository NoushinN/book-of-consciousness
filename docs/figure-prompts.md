# Suggested Figure Prompts and Captions

## Figure 1: Global Workspace Architecture
**Prompt:** Academic schematic diagram of Global Workspace Theory showing multiple unconscious specialized processors feeding into an attentional selection system, then broadcasting into a central global workspace connected to memory, language, decision-making, and action systems. Clean textbook style, no decorative elements.

**Caption:** Global Workspace Theory conceptualizes consciousness as globally broadcast information made available to multiple cognitive systems.

## Figure 2: Integrated Information Theory
**Prompt:** Minimal network diagram showing a system with highly integrated nodes compared to a disconnected modular system. Use labels such as integration, differentiation, causal structure, and system boundary.

**Caption:** Integrated Information Theory links consciousness to the degree and structure of integrated information within a system.

## Figure 3: Predictive Processing
**Prompt:** Hierarchical brain model showing top-down predictions and bottom-up prediction errors across sensory, perceptual, and abstract cognitive levels.

**Caption:** Predictive processing explains perception as a dynamic interaction between top-down expectations and bottom-up error correction.

## Figure 4: Recurrent Processing
**Prompt:** Visual cortex-style diagram showing feedforward sensory processing and recurrent feedback loops between cortical levels.

**Caption:** Recurrent Processing Theory argues that feedback interactions are central to conscious perception.

## Figure 5: Comparative Theory Map
**Prompt:** Academic concept map grouping consciousness theories into neuroscientific, computational, philosophical, embodied, and physical approaches.

**Caption:** Major theories of consciousness differ in explanatory target, empirical grounding, and metaphysical commitments.
